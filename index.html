Mastering Expressions the Way Native Speakers Do

Now I am getting hanging on this

This phrase isn't a standard native English expression. It's a direct, literal translation from a common Brazilian Portuguese phrase. The key here is to find the correct equivalent that a native speaker would use.

Context: Imagine you're in a meeting with your boss, who asks for a quick status update on a task. You've hit a roadblock and can't find the solution.

Portuguese: Chefe, me desculpe, mas estou ficando empacado com isso.

I'm stuck on this, sir.

  • "Now I am getting": While grammatically correct, it's not the natural way to express the current state. Native speakers would use a simpler, more direct verb.
  • "hanging on this": This is the literal translation of "empacado". The native equivalent is "stuck," which conveys the idea of being unable to move forward on a task.
